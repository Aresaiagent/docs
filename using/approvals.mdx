Ares is built on a simple rule: routine work runs on its own, and anything customer-visible or money-related waits for you.

## What requires approval

- Outbound messages beyond your standing follow-up rules
- Launching or materially changing ad campaigns, including any new spend
- New ad copy and creative, which you always see before it runs
- Anything Ares itself judges as risky or unusual for your account

## What runs without approval

- Reading and organizing CRM data
- Routine lead follow-up you have already authorized
- Reporting, monitoring, and analysis
- Pausing something that is clearly burning money

## Working the queue

Pending approvals appear on your dashboard. Each one shows what Ares wants to do, why, and exactly what will go out. You approve or reject with one tap, and you can reply in chat to ask for changes first: "shorten this message" or "lower that budget to $40 a day."

Approvals do not expire silently. If something sits unanswered, Ares reminds you rather than acting on its own.

<Note>
Approval settings are guardrails, not busywork. Most owners clear the queue in under a minute a day, and everything customer-facing stays under your control.
</Note>
