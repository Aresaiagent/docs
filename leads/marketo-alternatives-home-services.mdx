Home services businesses need instant lead response, AI qualification, and 24/7 booking - capabilities Marketo was never built for. Ares replies to inbound leads in seconds and books the estimate automatically.

Marketo is defined as a B2B marketing automation platform built for enterprise email nurture and 60-180 day sales cycles, not phone-driven local service work. Speed to lead refers to the gap between a lead's form submission and a business's first response - the single biggest predictor of booking rate in home services.

## Why does Marketo fail home services businesses?

Marketo was built for enterprise deals with long committees and long cycles, not the phone calls and same-day bookings home services runs on.

The platform syncs leads in 5-15 minute batches. A homeowner submits a form at 9:47 AM; Marketo processes it at 10:02 AM; a competitor already booked the job at 9:53 AM. Batch processing kills conversion when speed decides who wins.

Marketo also charges roughly \$1,200-\$6,000 per month for automation built around email nurture as the primary channel. Home services converts on fast conversations, not drip campaigns, so that spend buys attribution and account-based marketing modules a local shop will never use.

## What do home services businesses actually need from marketing automation?

Four capabilities separate a fit from a mismatch:

- **Response speed over open rates.** A lead answered within 60 seconds books far more often than one left until the next business day. Ares texts every new lead instantly and qualifies the homeowner by AI conversation, even at 11 PM on a Saturday.
- **Ad-to-conversation handoff.** Meta lead forms and web forms should flow straight into the CRM with no manual export-import step. Ares picks up new HighLevel leads instantly and opens a text conversation.
- **Mobile-first outreach.** Homeowners searching "emergency plumber near me" respond fastest by text, not a desktop form. Ares meets them with SMS and a one-tap booking link.
- **Repeat-business triggers.** Ares can run reactivation sequences in HighLevel - a maintenance reminder months later with a one-click rebooking link - so past customers return without manual effort.

## How does Ares deliver instant AI follow-up and booking for home services?

Ares is an AI operator that works on top of your GoHighLevel (HighLevel) CRM and Meta ad account, sitting between your lead sources and your calendar to handle response, qualification, and booking in real time.

A lead arrives from a Meta lead form or your website. Ares picks it up from HighLevel within seconds, sends an instant SMS, and asks what service is needed, the address, and whether it's an emergency. It then books the estimate or hands the lead to your team with full context.

Ares never sleeps - an 11 PM lead gets the same instant response as an 11 AM lead - and it manages multiple HighLevel sub-accounts for franchise or multi-market operations, so no market goes unanswered overnight. Every message respects opt-outs and consent, and sensitive actions wait for approval.

Ares costs \$299 per month flat, with \$100 per seat for larger enterprise teams - simple pricing next to Marketo's contact-based tiers.

## HubSpot Marketing Hub: unified CRM for growing home services teams

HubSpot combines marketing automation with native CRM and service ticketing. For businesses outgrowing spreadsheets but not ready for ServiceTitan's complexity, it offers a middle path: a free CRM tier for contacts and pipelines, and Marketing Hub Professional starting at \$800 per month for landing pages, ad management, and workflow automation.

It integrates with QuickBooks, Calendly, and Zapier, and setup takes days rather than Marketo's enterprise cycle. The tradeoff is speed - HubSpot workflows batch on 5-10 minute intervals, fine for email nurture but a loser in emergency categories like plumbing or HVAC repair.

HubSpot fits businesses that prioritize CRM consolidation over sub-minute response, especially those wanting marketing, sales, and service in one platform.

## ServiceTitan: field-service-native platform with built-in marketing

ServiceTitan is the category-defining software for residential home services, combining dispatch, invoicing, inventory, and marketing in one system. Unlike Marketo, a marketing tool, or Ares, a follow-up and booking layer, ServiceTitan is the operational system of record.

Marketing Pro, its automation module, triggers email and direct mail off job history already in the platform - a furnace installed in 2019 gets a year-five maintenance reminder automatically. Native booking widgets and reputation management pull Google LSA and Yelp leads directly into dispatch boards.

Pricing starts around \$245-\$500 per tech per month, with multi-year contracts common; a five-person team runs \$15,000-\$30,000 annually, covering dispatch, invoicing, payroll, and marketing together. It fits established businesses running 10+ technicians with complex inventory or multi-location operations, and replaces Marketo entirely - but demands operational commitment well beyond marketing automation.

## ActiveCampaign: lean email automation for service reminders

ActiveCampaign runs \$149 per month for up to 2,500 contacts, roughly a sixth of Marketo's entry price. For businesses focused on email retention - seasonal reminders, anniversary campaigns, referral requests - it covers the need without enterprise overhead.

Workflows trigger on customer actions or dates: a homeowner books gutter cleaning, gets tagged "gutter-annual," and receives a mid-season check-in and a rebooking campaign a year out. It integrates with Salesforce and Pipedrive, plus Zapier for tools like Jobber, syncing 1-5 minutes behind the trigger - faster than Marketo, slower than an instant AI responder.

ActiveCampaign lacks real-time lead response and AI qualification. It handles post-sale nurture well but doesn't replace instant lead-to-booking for acquisition; pair it with Ares for inbound response, or run it standalone if referrals drive more volume than paid ads.

## How do you choose the right platform for your home services business?

Match the tool to your acquisition motion. If Meta ads and web forms drive most leads and you compete on response speed, Ares wins. If referrals and repeat business dominate, ActiveCampaign handles nurture at low cost.

Evaluate existing systems first. Shops already on ServiceTitan should use its native Marketing Pro module rather than layering on separate automation. Ares layers onto HighLevel, so teams already there add instant AI follow-up without switching CRMs.

Weigh cost against job value. A high-ticket category like HVAC replacement or roofing justifies the spend, since one extra booked job a month covers the annual cost many times over; lower-ticket services may prefer ActiveCampaign's smaller fee.

Test it. Route half your Meta leads through instant automation and half through your current process, then measure booking rate. A double-digit lift is common when response time drops from 10+ minutes to under a minute.
