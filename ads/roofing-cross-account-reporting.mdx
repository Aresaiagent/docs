## Why does cross-location reporting break at traditional roofing agencies?

Most roofing agencies run each location as a separate account with a separate tracking stack. Five location managers. Five monthly PDFs. Five attribution models that never agree. Nobody can tell whether Phoenix is outperforming Dallas due to creative, targeting, or demand, because the numbers never sit side by side. That's the actual problem.

Cross-account reporting is defined as tracking leads, response times, and bookings across multiple HighLevel locations and Meta ad accounts inside one unified view. Ares treats a multi-location roofing operation as one view with per-location breakouts. Every lead and booked estimate across your markets lands in a single dashboard, so markets compare on the same terms. Because Ares is the operator responding to and booking leads at each location on your HighLevel CRM and Meta ad account, the reporting is a byproduct of the work, not a separate project you commission after the fact.

Agencies like Hook Agency and Thrive offer multi-location PPC management, but their reporting stays siloed by portal. You log into the ad platform for paid search, the CRM for pipeline, and a call-tracking tool for phone leads. Ares gives you the unified view agencies have promised for years but rarely ship.

## How does Ares give you one view across roofing locations?

Ares shows every location as a row in one dashboard: leads received, how fast each was answered, conversations in progress, and estimates booked. A roofing company running 30 locations sees 30 rows. Not 30 logins. HQ runs the network from one place instead of chasing exports.

The dashboard surfaces locations that need attention. If a market's response time slips or its booking rate drops week over week, it stands out before the franchise owner calls asking why. This is visibility and fast reaction, not a black-box model - Ares works inside the playbook and approvals you set.

Ares tracks each lead through to a booked estimate and hands off to your HighLevel pipeline from there. Long roofing sales cycles - estimate, then adjuster, then close - stay visible per market, so a slower-closing location does not get penalized against a faster one.

## What should multi-location roofing companies look for in reporting?

A fleet dashboard is defined as a single live screen showing every location's lead volume, response speed, and booked estimates side by side, updated live. Three things separate real multi-location capability from an agency that just runs more accounts:

- **One live view.** Every location shows up on the same screen, updating as leads arrive, not a monthly PDF assembled by hand. Ares shows leads, response time, and bookings by location in a single dashboard.
- **Per-location consistency.** Every market gets the same fast, on-brand response. Ares runs the same operator playbook everywhere, so a lead in Austin gets answered as fast as a lead in San Antonio. 24/7. Regardless of who is on shift.
- **HQ rollup.** Leadership sees the whole footprint and can drill into any single market. Ares rolls up the network and lets you filter to one location without a custom export.

## When does a traditional agency still make sense?

If you run one or two roofing locations and mainly need done-for-you creative, landing pages, local SEO, and Google Business Profile builds, a specialized roofing agency may get you launched faster and handle production work Ares does not do. Ares is not a creative studio. It is not a listings-management tool either.

Ares becomes the stronger choice as locations multiply and manual reporting breaks down. At that scale, the win is consistency and visibility: every location answers and books leads instantly, and you see all of them in one view. For companies scaling through acquisition, adding a location means connecting its HighLevel and Meta accounts and letting Ares start working its leads - not scoping a multi-month migration.

## How do you evaluate AI marketing for roofing?

Ask any AI marketing provider three questions before signing.

**First:** "Show me a live dashboard across multiple locations." If they cannot show a real multi-location view with real data, it is vaporware. Ares can walk you through the actual dashboard, not a mockup.

**Second:** "How do you handle roofing conversion events, since a booked estimate is not a closed job?" Roofing attribution is not ecommerce. A lead that books an estimate in March may not close until an adjuster visits in May. Ares books the estimate and tracks it through your HighLevel pipeline, so later stages stay visible per location.

**Third:** "What actually happens to my leads at 11 PM?" The point of an AI operator: every lead gets answered immediately, at every location, without a human refreshing tabs. Ares responds by SMS within seconds and works toward a booked estimate around the clock, inside the approvals and compliance rules you set.

## How do you set up cross-location reporting in Ares?

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect each location's accounts">
    Connect every location's HighLevel CRM and Meta ad account. Ares also runs Google Ads and manages each Google Business Profile. Each location's spend and data stay separated.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm your playbook">
    Set how leads should be greeted, what offers are allowed, and what counts as a qualified booking. Every location runs the same playbook. Sensitive actions wait for your approval.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Group your locations">
    Organize locations the way you run the business: by region, or by ownership type, company-owned versus franchisee, so the rollup matches how you report internally.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Watch the fleet dashboard">
    HQ watches every location in one view and drills into any location that needs attention. Slipping markets surface on their own. No manual review required.
  </Step>
</Steps>
